Nicoll Environmental Lab

❮ ❯

The James C. Nicoll Laboratory is a fairly new lab on campus. It houses multiple offices for ocean, civil, and environmental engineering faculty. The Nicoll Laboratory also handles many different research projects and contains a waste treatment resource center.

Navigation:

Nicoll is by far the hardest building to find, unless you know where to look. This laboratory is enclosed between the long Davidson Laboratory and the athletic field. The easiest way by far to get to this building is to walk up 8th as if you are going to Jonas Hall, and then when you get to the gates for the athletic parking lot, you walk through to the pathway to the right, and as you walk along the pathway, you will see the Nicoll Lab sign. If you are closer to 5th street, you can walk up and then when you get to River Street turn left, which looks like a dead end, but if you walk past the tall glass building on the left side, there is a metal set of stairs. When you get up the stairs and walk a little bit, you will see the sign for the Nicoll Lab.
